# Pump-solver baseline — high-power fold tail

**Goal.** Freeze the runtime of the existing pump-solver backends on the
high-power tail before testing new (Schur / matrix-free) backends, so any new
backend is judged against a fixed reference. The decision point is the
harmonic-balance **fold** near −22 dBm, not the easy low-power region.

**Setup.** IPM JTWPA design (`outputs/ipm_python_design`), pump 7.0 GHz,
positive-odd-jc basis (10 modes), nt=40, 2× JC pump-current convention. Warm-
started power sweep (cold continuation at the lowest power, then a single
full-scale Newton solve warm-started from the previous power). exp09 gain run
after every converged pump solve (signal 6.0 GHz, 10 sidebands). Generated by
`experiments/benchmark_exp08_pump_schur_matrixfree.py`
(`--backends full_mean_tangent full_real_coupled full_adaptive`); raw rows in
`outputs/pump_solver_backends/baseline_tail.csv`.

## What makes the fold expensive

The cost is `GMRES_total × matvec`. Toward the fold the harmonic-balance
Jacobian goes near-singular, so two things grow together:

| pump power | Newton | GMRES (mean_tangent) | GMRES/Newton |
|---|---|---|---|
| −33 dBm | 2 | 9 | 4.5 |
| −25 dBm | 4 | 49 | 12 |
| −22 dBm | **8** | **207** | **26** |

`mean_tangent` (cheap block-diagonal preconditioner) lets GMRES blow up;
`real_coupled` (exact full-Jacobian LU) holds GMRES at 1/Newton but pays a
~0.3 s LU **every Newton step**. Neither reaches sub-1 s at the fold.

## Baseline runtime at the fold (pump solve, seconds)

| power | full_mean_tangent | full_real_coupled | full_adaptive |
|---|---|---|---|
| −23.0 dBm | 1.10 | 1.76 | 1.01 |
| −22.5 dBm | 1.56 | 2.24 | 2.19 |
| −22.0 dBm | 3.21 | **3.51** | 3.48 |

* `full_real_coupled` is the **accepted fold baseline**: ~**3.51 s/point at
  −22 dBm**, GMRES = 1/Newton, exp09 gain 9.765 dB.
* `full_mean_tangent` is cheaper in the shoulder but its GMRES runs away (207 at
  −22); on this machine the smaller retained system masks it, but on the full
  6446-node problem it is the dominant fold cost.
* `full_adaptive` (mean_tangent below −22.7 dBm, fresh real_coupled at/above)
  is the current best *rule*: it takes the cheaper of the two per power, but at
  −22 it inherits real_coupled's 3.6 s.

## Negative result carried forward: factor reuse

Reusing the `real_coupled` LU across Newton steps (modified-Newton
preconditioning) was prototyped and **rejected**: near the fold the Jacobian
changes substantially between Newton steps, so a reused factor preconditions
poorly and GMRES explodes (123/Newton at −22, pump 25 s vs 3.7 s fresh). It is
recorded only as a negative comparison; it is not a candidate.

## Acceptance reference

All baselines converge to coeff_rel < 1e-9 and agree on exp09 gain to the
printed digit (0 dB drift among themselves). A new backend must match this gain
to **< 0.01 dB** and beat **3.51 s at −22 dBm** to be worthwhile; the stretch
target is **sub-1 s/point**.
