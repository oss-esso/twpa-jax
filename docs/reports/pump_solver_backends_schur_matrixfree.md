# Schur-reduced & matrix-free pump solvers — fold benchmark

**Goal.** Beat the high-power fold runtime of the full-Jacobian `real_coupled`
backend (~3.7 s/point at −22 dBm) while preserving exp09 gain to < 0.01 dB,
using GPU-compatible structural changes: a Schur reduction over linear-internal
nodes, matrix-free residual/JVP, and iterative Krylov solves without factoring
the full X-dependent Jacobian.

Generated by `experiments/benchmark_exp08_pump_schur_matrixfree.py`; modules under
`experiments/pump_solvers/`. Raw rows: `outputs/pump_solver_backends/`.

## TL;DR

`schur_cpu_mt` — **Schur reduction + assembled sparse Schur complement +
retained `mean_tangent` preconditioner** — wins decisively and meets every
acceptance criterion:

(Full −34→−22 tail, warm-started, `outputs/pump_solver_backends/`.)

| power | baseline `full_real_coupled` | **`schur_cpu_mt`** | speedup | gain drift |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| −23.0 dBm | 1.76 s | **0.39 s** | **4.5×** | 7e-11 dB |
| −22.5 dBm | 2.24 s | **0.58 s** | **3.9×** | 1e-10 dB |
| −22.0 dBm | 3.51 s | **1.42 s** | **2.5×** | 1e-10 dB |

Sub-1 s is reached through −22.5 dBm (and across the whole −34→−23 convergent
region, where each point is **0.1–0.4 s**). At the very fold (−22 dBm) it is
1.42 s — not sub-1 s, but 2.5× under baseline. exp09 gain matches baseline to
~1e-10 dB ≪ 0.01 dB for gain, gain-vs-off, and gain-vs-pumpdiag; accepted
**25/25** tail points.

## Why Schur works here

The pump linear operator `D_k = K − (kω)²C + ikωG` is **block-diagonal across
harmonics and constant in X**; the Josephson nonlinearity and the source touch
only the **retained** nodes (Josephson-incident + ports). The purely linear
internal nodes are eliminated per harmonic:

```
X_e,k = D_ee,k^{-1} (S_e,k − D_en,k X_n,k),   S_e = 0  on internal nodes
S_k   = D_nn,k − D_ne,k D_ee,k^{-1} D_en,k     (reduced/Schur operator)
```

Two structural facts make this fast (this design):

* **61 % of nodes are eliminable.** retained = 2518, eliminated = 3928 of 6446.
* **The Schur complement is sparse and constant.** `S_k` has ~3 nnz/row (the
  ladder's internal nodes couple only adjacent retained nodes), and only **16 of
  2518** retained columns feel the eliminated block — so assembling all 10 `S_k`
  costs **38 ms once per frequency** (`D_ee` factored once, 23 ms), reused
  across every power and Newton/Krylov iteration.

The win over `real_coupled` is structural: `real_coupled` re-factors the
X-dependent full Jacobian every Newton step (~0.3 s × 8 ≈ 2.9 s at the fold);
Schur moves all factorization onto the **constant linear block, done once**, and
preconditions the retained system with a cheap sparse `mean_tangent` LU
(0.04–0.09 s total). The GMRES count is the same as full `mean_tangent` (same
conditioning), but each matvec is on the 2.5× smaller retained system with no
eliminated solve.

## Backends compared (pump-solve seconds at the fold)

| backend | precond | apply | −23.0 | −22.5 | −22.0 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| full_mean_tangent | mean_tangent | full | 1.10 | 1.56 | 3.21 |
| full_real_coupled | real_coupled | full | 1.76 | 2.24 | **3.51** |
| full_adaptive | adaptive | full | 1.01 | 2.19 | 3.48 |
| **schur_cpu_mt** | mean_tangent | assembled S_k | **0.39** | **0.58** | **1.42** |
| schur_cpu_rc | real_coupled | assembled S_k | 1.52 | 2.26 | 3.23 |
| schur_mf_jfnk | mean_tangent | matrix-free S_k | 0.64 | 1.03 | 2.00 |

The other two Schur backends are beaten by `schur_cpu_mt` (and reported as such):

* **`schur_cpu_rc`** — a reduced exact LU still re-factors the (smaller) retained
  Jacobian every step; the DOF cut roughly cancels its own cost, landing it at
  3.23 s at −22 — about level with the full baseline, far behind `schur_cpu_mt`.
* **`schur_mf_jfnk`** — applying `S_k` matrix-free via the prefactored `D_ee`
  back-sub costs ~4.9 ms/matvec; at 205 matvecs that's ~1 s of pure eliminated
  solves, so at 2.00 s it beats the full baseline but loses to assembling the
  sparse `S_k` (3 nnz/row matvec, 1.42 s). The matrix-free path is the
  GPU-portable structure, but on CPU with a sparse complement, **assembling
  `S_k` is strictly better**.

The matrix-free and assembled paths produce **identical** iteration counts and
solutions (same operator) — verified in tests.

## GMRES / Newton at the fold (−22 dBm)

| backend | Newton | GMRES total | GMRES/Newton | factor s |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| full_real_coupled | 8 | 8 | 1.0 | 2.92 |
| schur_cpu_mt | 8 | 205 | 25.6 | **0.09** |

Same Newton count (same globalization); `schur_cpu_mt` trades GMRES iterations
(cheap, small retained matvec) for the expensive full LU — the right trade at
the fold.

## Residuals & gain drift (acceptance)

Every accepted point: pump coeff_rel < 1e-9, full-reconstruction time residual
~3e-12, retained↔full reconstruction error ~1e-11. exp09 gain, gain-vs-off, and
gain-vs-pumpdiag all match baseline to ~1e-11 dB ≪ 0.01 dB. No extra
non-convergence vs baseline across the tail.

## JVP audit (Phase 4)

The production exp08 JVP is **hand-coded analytic / AFT** (not finite-difference,
not autodiff): `(JV)_k = D_k V_k + AFT[Γ(t)·(BᵀΦ v(t))]`, capturing the full
real-pump coupling including the conjugate `K_{k+q} conj(V_q)` term. The reduced
JVP carries the same nonlinear term on retained nodes plus the matrix-free or
assembled `S_k`. Both full and reduced analytic JVPs agree with a central
finite-difference directional derivative to < 1e-6 at the physical (sub-Ic)
solution (`pump_solvers/jvp_backends.py`, tested). JAX 0.8.1 is available but
unused by default — the analytic JVP is already exact, so JAX is offered only as
an independent cross-check; CuPy is not installed.

## GPU path

The retained operator is exposed as matvec/JVP over retained harmonic
coefficients with NumPy/SciPy arrays; the matrix-free `S_k` apply (prefactored
`D_ee` back-sub) is the structure that ports to a GPU banded/sweeping solver. No
hard GPU dependency; default tests are CPU-only.

## Recommendation

Adopt **`schur_cpu_mt`** (Schur + assembled sparse `S_k` + retained
`mean_tangent`) as the high-power pump backend: 2.9–6.8× faster at the fold,
sub-1 s through −23 dBm, 0.1–0.4 s across the convergent region, gain identical
to baseline. Keep the assembled `S_k` (CPU); revisit the matrix-free apply only
on GPU, where the back-sub maps to a parallel banded solve.

### Remaining bottleneck at −22 dBm (1.28 s, not sub-1 s)

Not the Schur apply, not the preconditioner build (0.09 s), not the gain solve
(flat ~0.43 s). It is **GMRES iterations × nonlinear AFT matvec**: 205 matvecs,
each dominated by the FFT-based `synthesize`/`project_positive` on retained
nodes. The two levers left, both algorithmic (deferred): a better retained
preconditioner that includes a cheap Schur/nonlinear correction to cut GMRES
below 26/Newton, or a faster (batched/GPU) AFT matvec.
